Dragon Boat Festival

The Festival is a legendary holiday usually celebrated on the fifth day of the fifth lunar month. On this day, people race dragon boats in honor of the ancient patriotic poet Qu Yuan and to ward off diseases and misfortunes. The festival is also accompanied by traditions like eating zongzi (sticky rice dumplings), hanging up mugwort leaves, and other customs that create a joyful atmosphere.

Tomb Sweeping Day

Qingming Festival, also known as Tomb Sweeping Day, is a day for paying respects to ancestors and deceased loved ones. Typically observed on the fourth or fifth day of the fourth lunar month, people clean the graves, make offerings to their ancestors, and engage in environmentally friendly activities such as tree planting to show respect and gratitude to nature.
